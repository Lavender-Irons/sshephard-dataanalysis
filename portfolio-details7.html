Overview

Almost every Friday, my grandmother wanted to have Dominos, I would order this on my phone via Google browser on my android phone. I am continuously logged in but when placing my order, it does not allow me to save the location site. I have to enter the zip code each order, find the store, then choose this store to place order.

Due to Coronavirus, Dominos offers car side delivery. This is a great service, but each time I have to enter the color, make, model of my car and preferred location where I would like them to leave the order. I am also unable to save this information.

After asking workers at Dominos if they have had others express that it would be advantageous to have save button for these two items, I engaged in this redesign as an opportunity to improve the experience.

Through usability testing, I discovered many users who stated that they would utilize the save button and it would expedite placing their orders. Based on my research findings, I designed solutions and validated these changes with additional usability tests.

Disclaimer: I am not affiliated with Dominos Pizza in any capacity.

Users

Target Audience Demographics

According to Forbes, Dominos target audience is very broad due to Dominos diverse consumer base; however, they do focus on consumers between the ages of 18-35. In addition, they focus on targeting lower to middle class couples where both the husband and wife are working because Dominos provides a quick and easy meal for families.

In 2015, Dominos AnyWare was created, it is a suite of ordering technology that gives customers 15 digital ways to order. This has transitioned to an e-commerce based profile. In 2019, Dominos generated more than 65% of sales via digital ordering channels in the U.S.

Research

Guerilla Usability Testing

To test the website’s user experience, I surveyed people arriving at Dominos Pizza to pick up their pizza. I asked them if they used the online Dominos website to order their food. I asked them to go through placing an order and took notes.

    During this usability test, comments made were:
  • "I hate having to enter my car information every time."
  • "I know this probably saves when using the Dominos app, but I can’t download the app right now. My phone is out of memory."
  • "I do not live here but I pass through every few weeks, I wish I can save this location for ordering."

    Survey

    I created an online survey that asked questions regarding the current Dominos website. It asked 200 individuals, focusing on individuals of the targeted demographics. The survey and results as follows:

    Survey Results

    Based on the online survey, 77% of individuals have used the website to order from Dominos, with 71% using store pick up. When entering store information, 30% found entering the information each time, helpful or very helpful. Sixty-one percent used car side delivery, only 39% found entering the car information each time helpful or very helpful. When asked if they would use a saved feature, 96% would used some type of saved feature.
